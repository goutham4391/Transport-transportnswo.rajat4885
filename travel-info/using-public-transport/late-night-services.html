Late night services

In New South Wales, there are public transport options throughout the night and early morning. You can use the Trip Planner or a transport app to plan your late night travel.

Regional train and coach services - overnight travel

Some NSW TrainLink Regional Trains travel overnight on long distance services in NSW and interstate. You can upgrade to a First Class Sleeper on certain services.

Sydney

  • A number of bus routes operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
  • NightRide buses replace most train services between midnight and 4.30am, stopping at railways stations or on main roads near railway stations.
  • Some regular bus routes run later services after midnight on weekends only.
  • Some Intercity train services run after midnight.
  • The last service on some ferry routes depart between midnight and 1am.

Metro

You can catch Sydney Metro night services 7 days a week.

Plan ahead using the Trip Planner and transport apps.

Train

From midnight to 4.30am, most trains stop running. NightRide buses replace Sydney train services between midnight and 4:30am, stopping near stations along the train line.

Some Intercity train services run after midnight

Many NightRide services leave the city from bus stops near Town Hall Station.

You can also view the route and stopping sequence for NightRide services on the Sydney NightRide bus network map (pdf 342KB) or download a map (pdf 1.2MB) showing where you can catch a NightRide bus along with other late night travel options across the Sydney CBD.

Ferry

Some ferries run the last service between midnight and 1am. See timetables or use the Trip Planner to find out when the last ferry to your destination leaves.

Find a timetable or plan your trip.

Light rail

From Saturday 12 February 2022, L2 and L3 light rail vehicles will run on the L1 Dulwich Hill Line every 15 minutes, Monday to Sunday 6am to 11pm (midnight on Friday). Services will run between Dulwich Hill and Central Station. Find out more.

Route L2 light rail services run between Circular Quay and Randwick via the CBD, 7 days a week starting at 5am and finishing at 1am the following day.

Route L3 light rail services run between Circular Quay and Kingsford via the CBD, 7 days a week starting at 5am and finishing at 1am the following day.

Find a timetable or plan your trip.

Taxi

On Friday and Saturday nights, plus nights before public holidays, wait for taxis at a secure taxi rank. Secure taxi ranks are pre-paid between 10pm and 5am, so you may be asked to pay up front, before your trip begins.

Find a secure taxi rank location or for city locations you can download this map (pdf 1.2MB).

Newcastle

All late night services in the Opal network accept Opal cards and most are wheelchair accessible.

Extra late night buses run on weekends with services around local hot spots.

Bus

The NightOwl buses run after midnight and throughout the early hours of the morning in Newcastle on weekends.

NightOwl bus services run on Friday and Saturday nights only.
