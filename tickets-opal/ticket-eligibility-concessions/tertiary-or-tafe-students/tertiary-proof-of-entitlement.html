Tertiary proof of entitlement requirements

To travel on concession fares on public transport in NSW, you must be able to show proof of entitlement.

Tertiary student ID cards from many tertiary institutions are recognised as proof of entitlement when travelling within the Opal network. However there are some that are not.

If your tertiary student card is not recognised as proof of entitlement, you will need to apply for a Transport Concession Entitlement Card. This is valid on public transport across NSW.

For travel within Sydney and surrounding areas, all tertiary students need to apply for a Concession Opal card with their proof of entitlement card information.

Find your tertiary institution to confirm your proof of entitlement requirements.

Institutions issuing an approved form of Student ID

Eligible tertiary students enrolled at the following institutions can use their Student photo ID card as proof of entitlement when travelling with a Concession Opal card in the Opal network.

You only need to apply for a Transport Concession Entitlement Card if you are travelling in rural or regional NSW.
