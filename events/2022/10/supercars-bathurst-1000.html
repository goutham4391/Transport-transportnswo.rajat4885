Important travel advice

Motor sport fans are encouraged to plan ahead and consider all the available travel options. Allow extra travel time on the roads as traffic will be heavier than usual, you can find real-time updates on traffic conditions at livetraffic.com.

You’ll need to use an Opal card or contactless payment card/device to travel on public transport. Register your details online at Opal or via the app ahead of time.

Ticketing information

Opal

You can use an Opal card for all modes of public transport in Sydney and surrounding regions, including metro, trains, buses, ferries and light rail.

Remember to top up your Opal card before you travel to avoid waiting in line at top-up machines. You can top up your card online or at an Opal retailer.

More ways to pay

If you have a contactless-enabled American Express, Mastercard or Visa debit or credit card, or a linked device, you can use it to pay for your travel by tapping on and tapping off at Opal card readers. Available on all public transport, contactless payments are another way to pay on the Opal network. Adult Opal fare pricing applies and you will receive Opal benefits, including daily and weekly fare caps.

Metro

Sydney Metro services are high-frequency, driverless trains running between Tallawong Station and Chatswood Station.

Please note, from Monday 3 to Friday 7 October Metro North West services run between Tallawong and North Ryde and From Saturday 8 to Sunday 9 October buses replace services between Tallawong and Chatswood due to rail upgrade works. See the trackwork news story for more info.  You may need to change for a bus or train to continue your trip, plan ahead using the Trip Planner.

For Metro service disruption information, check trackwork and travel alerts or plan your trip using the Trip Planner.

Train

The closest station to the race track is Bathurst on the Blue Mountains Line. Regular NSW TrainLink services run from Sydney to Lithgow with coach connections to Bathurst.

The ‘Bathurst Bullet’ train service links Sydney with Bathurst, with services running twice daily. For further information see regional trains.

Bus

Bathurst Buslines will be operating buses from most areas of Bathurst, Orange and Lithgow to Mount Panorama Race Track. Bus tickets are not included in your event ticket. Bus tickets can be purchased from the bus driver.

  • Route 771 - Bathurst Town to Mount Panorama and return
  • Route 772 - Kelso & CBD to Mount Panorama and return
  • Route 773 - Town Motel Accommodation to Mount Panorama and return
  • Route 775 - Skyline Night Time Shuttle to Town and return

For more details on bus routes and timetables visit Bathurst Buslines - Bathurst 1000 page.

Bathurst Buslines will also operate continual shuttle services from Harris Park at the bottom of Mount Panorama to Reid Park at top of Mount Panorama each day - return tickets available from Ticket Booth at bottom of Mountain in Merchandise area. For further information please contact Bathurst Buslines on 02 6331 2744. 
 

Services from Orange and Lithgow

  • On Friday, Saturday and Sunday, return services will run from Orange stopping at Lucknow, Vittoria and The Rocks to Mount Panorama.
  • On Saturday and Sunday, return services will run from Lithgow stopping at Wallerawang, Portland, Meadow Flat and Yetholme

For information visit lithgowbuslines.com.au or phone 02 6352 3888.

Camping shuttle service

Bathurst Buslines will run a shuttle bus service to and from Lloyds Road for those staying in the Orchard Campground. The shuttle bus will operate each day of the event.

Accessible Shuttle

An accessible shuttle bus will be available each day of the event from 8.30am to 6.30pm. Call Bathurst Buslines on 0408 482 951 to arrange pickup. The shuttle will pick up from the Viewing Platform 1 on the outside of Murray’s corner and drop off to the desired location. Once ready to return patrons will need to contact the shuttle again to be picked up and returned to the desired platform.

Drive

Delays are expected on major roads approaching Bathurst including the M4 Mwy, Great Western Hwy and Bells Line of Rd from Sydney, Mitchell Hwy from Orange, Mid-Western Hwy from Cowra and Castlereagh Hwy from Mudgee. 

Traffic controllers will be on-site on local roads to manage access to and from the Mount Panorama precinct. 

On Sunday when the event has finished, traffic will be heavy along the Great Western Hwy towards Sydney with significant eastbound delays expected through the Blue Mountains. Traffic will also be heavy on the Mid Western Hwy approaching Cowra and the Mitchell Hwy towards Orange.

Changed traffic conditions including reduced speed zones will be in place on the Great Western Hwy at Mount Victoria and the Mid Western Hwy at Cowra to manage traffic. 

Motorists should allow plenty of extra travel time over the weekend.

Note: Camping facilities are open from Saturday 1 October so traffic may be heavier than usual in the area leading up to the weekend.

For the latest traffic information visit Live Traffic NSW or download a real-time traffic app.

Taxi

An event taxi rank will be located on Lloyds Rd at Havannah St. For more details see the event site map. 

Book online with Bathurst Taxis. See the Bathurst Taxis booking page.

Parking

Priority parking is now sold out but there is plenty of general on-site parking is available, entry off Panorama Rd. See the Mount Panorama 1000 parking page for more details. 

Accessibility

Accessible Shuttle Bus Service

A wheelchair equipped Mini Bus will operate an On Demand Service as required from Murray’s Corner/Museum Car Park Via Under Track Tunnel. The service will be available on Thursday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday of the event from 8.30am to 5.30pm. Patrons must call Bathurst Buslines on 0408 482 951 to arrange pickup.

Transport

Accessible trips are shown in the Trip Planner with the wheelchair icon. You can select "accessible services only" in the Options to ensure you only see accessible services. Only trip plans with accessible train platforms, ferry wharves and buses will be presented. See accessible travel or download a transport app (select the "Accessibility" filter).
